The goal

Most self-driving projects rely on equipment that costs thousands of dollars. We challenged ourselves to build software that achieves the same basic goals using just a standard, budget-friendly camera.

How it works

Four steps.
Zero humans.

Every fraction of a second, the car runs through this loop entirely on its own.

01
Perception
The car sees the world

A forward-facing camera captures the road, lane markings, signs, and obstacles — dozens of times every second. AI vision models (the same kind used in smartphones) scan each frame and understand what's there.

Live perception feed
02
Understanding
AI makes sense of it

Neural networks — layers of math inspired by the human brain — process the raw camera data into meaning: where are the lanes? Is there a stop sign? Is something in the way?

03
Planning
It plots the best path

A routing brain (using the same algorithm Google Maps uses) calculates the optimal route across 90+ waypoints — accounting for road rules, intersections, and obstacles — in milliseconds.

04
Control
The car drives itself

The AI sends steering and throttle commands directly to the vehicle — no human in the loop. It holds its lane, accelerates smoothly, and stops when it needs to. All by itself.
